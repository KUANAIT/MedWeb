Diseases

Severe Acute Respiratory Syndrome (SARS)

SARS (Severe Acute Respiratory Syndrome) is a viral respiratory illness caused by the SARS-associated coronavirus (SARS-CoV). It was first identified in 2002 in southern China and led to a global outbreak in 2003. SARS primarily spreads through respiratory droplets when an infected person coughs or sneezes.

Symptoms of SARS include:

  • High fever (above 38째C)
  • Chills and body aches
  • Dry cough
  • Shortness of breath or difficulty breathing
  • Fatigue
  • Diarrhea (in some cases)

In severe cases, SARS can progress to pneumonia, which can be life-threatening. The outbreak was contained by 2004.

Monkeypox

Monkeypox is a viral zoonotic disease caused by the monkeypox virus, part of the Orthopoxvirus genus. It was first identified in 1958 in monkeys kept for research, and the first human case was recorded in 1970. The virus can spread from animals to humans and between humans through close contact.

Symptoms of monkeypox include:

  • Fever
  • Headache
  • Muscle aches and backache
  • Swollen lymph nodes
  • Chills
  • Exhaustion
  • A rash that often starts on the face and spreads to other parts of the body

The rash eventually forms scabs and falls off as part of the healing process. In severe cases, complications like secondary infections or pneumonia can occur. Monkeypox is similar to smallpox, but it is less severe and less contagious.

Coronavirus (COVID-19)

Coronavirus disease (COVID-19) is caused by the SARS-CoV-2 virus, a novel coronavirus that was first identified in Wuhan, China, in December 2019. It led to a global pandemic, causing widespread illness and disruptions. COVID-19 primarily spreads through respiratory droplets and can infect individuals through close contact or touching contaminated surfaces.

Symptoms of COVID-19 include:

  • Fever or chills
  • Dry cough
  • Shortness of breath or difficulty breathing
  • Fatigue
  • Muscle or body aches
  • Loss of taste or smell
  • Sore throat
  • Headache
  • Congestion or runny nose
  • Nausea or vomiting
  • Diarrhea

While most cases of COVID-19 result in mild to moderate respiratory illness, severe cases can lead to pneumonia, acute respiratory distress syndrome (ARDS), organ failure, or death, particularly in older adults and individuals with underlying health conditions. Vaccines and public health measures have played a key role in controlling the pandemic.

Heart Disease

Heart disease refers to a range of conditions that affect the heart, including coronary artery disease, heart rhythm problems (arrhythmias), and congenital heart defects. It is often caused by a combination of lifestyle factors, genetic predisposition, and other health conditions such as high blood pressure and diabetes.

Common symptoms of heart disease include:

  • Chest pain or discomfort
  • Shortness of breath
  • Fatigue
  • Palpitations or irregular heartbeats
  • Dizziness or light-headedness
  • Swelling in the legs, ankles, or feet

Managing heart disease often involves lifestyle changes, medication, and sometimes surgical interventions to reduce symptoms and improve quality of life.

Fever

A body temperature above 38째C (100.4째F) is generally considered a fever. Fevers are a common response of the body to infections and other illnesses. They can be caused by various factors including bacterial or viral infections, inflammatory conditions, and other medical issues.

Common causes of a temperature above 38째C include:

  • Infections such as the flu, cold, or COVID-19
  • Inflammatory diseases like rheumatoid arthritis
  • Heat exhaustion or heat stroke
  • Medication side effects or reactions

A fever itself is not usually dangerous, but it can be a sign of an underlying health issue. Persistent or very high fevers should be evaluated by a healthcare professional to determine the cause and appropriate treatment.
